# Security & Privacy Direction

This describes the policy direction we're building toward, not a
compliance claim — there is no product handling real user data yet.

## Principles

- User signal data is treated as sensitive by default, whether or not it
  technically qualifies as health data.
- Raw signal data is not sent to third-party AI models unless it's the
  minimum necessary for a specific, well-defined purpose. Where possible,
  data is abstracted or reduced before it reaches any external model.
- Users should be able to see what's collected, understand why, and turn
  it off without being penalized for doing so.
- AI outputs are treated as a layer that assists interpretation, not as
  unquestionable fact — critical logic should stay explainable rather than
  living entirely inside a model's output.

## Where We Are Now

This is a pre-MVP policy direction that will shape the system as it's
actually built, not a finished privacy program. A full privacy policy and
terms of service will be written and published ahead of any beta that
collects real user data.
